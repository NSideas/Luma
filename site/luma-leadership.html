Luma Leadership

Your team is experienced, knowledgeable, and credentialed, including CPAs, CFP® professionals and CFA charterholders. As part of a larger organization, we, and the many advisors at Fairport Asset Management, have been serving clients for over half a century – and remain dedicated to helping you grow your wealth to achieve your goals.

We take our commitment to helping empower women very seriously, and believe in giving back, both as a firm and individually, with many of our professionals deeply involved in community and women-focused organizations.

If you are an experienced advisor who would like to learn more about joining the Luma Wealth Advisors team, please send your resume and cover letter to careers@lumawealth.com. We are always looking for exceptional talent.

Heather R. Ettinger

Founder & Luma Wealth Advisor

Heather Ettinger has devoted more than 25 years to the financial services industry. She specializes in helping clients and their families create strategic financial plans to guide them through life transitions, such as the loss of a spouse, divorce and job changes. She is widely recognized for her unwavering dedication to helping other women build their financial acumen and philanthropic legacy.

Heather co-authored two studies about women and their unique needs entitled “Women of Wealth: Why Does the Financial Services Industry Still Not Hear Them?” and “Women of Wealth: What Do Breadwinner Women Want?” Heather has been featured in many publications including The Wall Street Journal, Barron’s and Bloomberg and is a frequently-requested speaker for both industry associations and company women’s initiatives. For her impact on the financial industry and demonstrated willingness to share her experiences, she was honored on InvestmentNews’inaugural “Woman to Watch” list in 2015.

Heather remains an ardent supporter of her alma maters including Dartmouth College, where she earned a B.A., and Laurel School for girls, where she now serves as an Emeritus Trustee. She is active with In Counsel with Women and the nationally recognized wealth management study group, Family Wealth Advisors Council. Currently, Heather serves on the Board of Directors of University Hospitals Health System and The Private Trust Company.

Heather lives in Shaker Heights, Ohio, with her husband, Jeff, and considers raising her three kids as her greatest accomplishment. In her free time, she can be found playing ice hockey, snow skiing and hiking.

An authentic change-maker and advocate, Heather’s theme song is “You Will (The OWN Song)” by Jennifer Hudson and Jennifer Nettles.

Kenneth J. Coleman

Luma Wealth Advisor

Kenneth Coleman provides more than 30 years of experience in the financial services industry including wealth management, banking and insurance. He leads in the areas of Human Resources, Compliance, Finance, Operations, Information Technology and Client Teams.

Because of his big picture perspective and growth mindset, Ken is a “go to” advisor for many business owners and executives. A champion for women advisors and clients for decades, Ken has been integral to the formation of Luma Wealth. He was a President of National City Bank’s Insurance Services Group and Senior Vice President with Capitol American.

Ken is an alumnus of Ashland University, where he earned a B.A. in Management and Economics and is a graduate of Leadership Cleveland, Class of 2015. Active in the local community, Ken is a member of the Midtown Cleveland, Inc. Emeritus Committee consisting of dedicated past Board Chairs and former trustees, as well as a member of the Greater Cleveland Partnership’s Government Affairs Council. He also serves on the board of a private family foundation and the board of a private manufacturing company.

Ken resides in Chagrin Falls, Ohio, with his wife, Jan, and has two children and two grandchildren. In his spare time, Ken can be found hiking, kayaking, watching sports and spending time with his grandkids.

An untiring manager of talent with a terrific sense of humor, Ken’s theme song is “Ain’t No Rest for the Wicked” by Cage the Elephant.

Emily A. Drake, CFP®, AEP®

Luma Wealth Advisor

Emily Drake is a Certified Financial PlannerTM with more than 30 years of industry experience. She is known not only for her in-depth financial expertise, but also for her empathy for the emotional aspects of financial decision making, especially in times of challenging transitions like the loss of a spouse or divorce.

Emily is being honored this year as a YWCA Greater Cleveland Woman of Achievement for extraordinary career success, community service, mentoring and dedication to the Y’s mission of eliminating racism and empowering women.

Emily held leadership positions at McDonald Trust Company and Ameritrust National Bank in Cleveland. Her career began in New York City with two Wall Street firms — Donaldson, Lufkin, Jenrette and Drexel Burnham Lambert. Emily holds a B.S. from Grand Valley State University and is a graduate of the National Trust Graduate School of Northwestern University. She holds a Certified Financial PlannerTM (CFP®) certification and an Accredited Estate Planner® (AEP®) designation.

She is passionate about advocating for older adults and caregivers and is the Chairperson of the Board for The Benjamin Rose Institute on Aging. She is an active member of the Saint Luke’s Foundation Board, a member of In Counsel With Women, the Planned Giving Council to the Cleveland Museum of Art, the Cleveland Estate Planning Council and she serves on the United Way of Greater Cleveland’s Investment Committee.

A resident of Cleveland Heights, Ohio, Emily enjoys entertaining, spending time outdoors with her dog, Bella, and staying active through hiking, biking, skiing and diving.

A woman who reaches back while she rises up, Emily’s theme song is Carley Simon’s “Let The River Run” from Working Girl.

Emily N. Shacklett, CPA/PFS, AEP®

Luma Wealth Advisor

Emily Shacklett is a CPA with more than 20 years of industry knowledge. She is known not only for her thorough tax and accounting expertise, but also for her warmth, attention to detail and entrepreneurial attitude. She has extensive experience in providing clients with clarity on complex tax-related matters and executive compensation/benefits and offering peace of mind through risk mitigation strategies.

Emily held leadership positions at Brockman Coats Gedelian & Co. and KPMG. She graduated with honors from the University of Akron with a B.S. in Accounting and is a member of the American Institute of CPAs and the Ohio Society of CPAs. She is a graduate of several local civic engagement and leadership programs including the YWCA Momentum program and Akron Torchbearers. Emily is also a member of the Union Club of Cleveland where she serves on the Membership and Retention Committees. She is a board member and current Vice President of the Cleveland Estate Planning Council where she serves on the membership and AEP committees and co-chairs the ESA committee. She is a supporter and volunteer at the Ronald McDonald House.

Emily resides in Richfield, Ohio with her family and in her free time she enjoys spending time outdoors, reading and supporting Cleveland sports.

A successful working mother of three, Emily’s theme song is “Superwoman” by Alicia Keys

Andrew R. Connors, CFP®, CEPA®

Luma Wealth Advisor

Andrew Connors is a Certified Financial PlannerTM with over 20 years of experience in the personal financial management arena. An animated and dynamic leader, Andrew engages others to be their best selves. His mission and purpose is to positively impact the long-term well-being of closely-held and family business owners by helping them create and maintain multigenerational wealth.

Andrew earned his B.S.B.A. from John Carroll University and MBA from the Weatherhead School of Management at Case Western Reserve University. He holds the Certified Financial Planner (CFP) certification from the College of Financial Planning, as well as the Certified Exit Planning Advisor (CEPA®) credential from the Exit Planning Institute.

An advocate for continuous improvement, Andrew is an alum of the Cleveland Leadership Center’s Bridge Builders civic engagement program and current participant of the exclusive Schwab Executive Leadership program comprised of national RIA leaders and faculty from top graduate schools. Andrew is the past Board President and a Trustee for Esperanza, Inc. He serves on the Executive and Advisory board of the John Carroll Entrepreneur’s Association, the Northeast Ohio Advisory Committee of the Exit Planning Institute and is a member of the Cleveland chapter of the Financial Planning Association.

Andrew and his wife, Anne-Marie, have twin boys. He balances his love for good food and wine with spending time outdoors and staying active through hiking, biking and fishing.

A professional focused on overcoming obstacles and maintaining forward momentum, Andrew’s theme song is “Carry On” by the band FUN.

Kristen T. Lucas

Luma Director of Marketing

Kristen Lucas brings nearly 15 years of industry experience to Luma Wealth, serving in client relationship and marketing capacities. She is responsible for the strategy, development and implementation of Luma’s communications and events. Known for her collaborative nature and creativity, she looks for unique and impactful ways to engage with clients and the community.

Kristen graduated Phi Beta Kappa from Allegheny College and holds a B.A. in English. She is an alumnae of the YWCA’s professional leadership Bootcamp and a YWCA Woman of Professional Excellence. Kristen is passionate about finding a cure for diabetes and fundraises as a team walk captain for local JDRF and ADA chapters. She is a supporter and founding friend’s committee member of United Cerebral Palsy of Greater Cleveland.

Kristen and her husband, Jeff, live in Cleveland Heights, Ohio, with their young daughter. Her hobbies include reading, yoga, visiting museums and spending time outdoors with her family.

An optimist through and through, Kristen’s theme song is “Unwritten” by Natasha Bedingfield.
